# Summary Report: "FCAJ Meetup – June 13, 2026"

### Event Objectives

- Share real-world experiences from engineers and professionals working at multinational companies (MNCs) and AWS.
- Provide career guidance and mentorship for students and junior professionals in the FCAJ community.
- Introduce career pathways from the FCAJ/FCJ program to AWS Community Builder and AWS Partners.

### Key Highlights

#### Speaker 1: Hoàng Trọng Trường – "What Does a DevOps Engineer Really Do?"

**Busting myths about DevOps:**
- Common perception: DevOps = person who writes CI/CD pipelines, manages Docker/Kubernetes, or deploys code at midnight.
- Reality: DevOps spans many domains — CI/CD pipelines, IaC, Container Orchestration (Kubernetes), Cloud Providers (AWS/Azure), and Logging & Monitoring.

**What to learn first:**
- **Fundamentals:** Linux, Networking basics, a programming language (Python or Go), Git and CI/CD.
- **Containers:** Understand how applications run — build, test, deploy, logs, configuration, and environment variables.
- **Build small projects:** Deploy a simple app, automate something, monitor it, break it, then fix it.

**DevOps tool landscape:**
- Exposure to the wide array of DevOps tools and why understanding fundamentals matters more than knowing every tool.

**Hard lessons learned:**
- Copying commands is not the same as understanding them.
- Learn to identify the real owner of a problem.
- Always ask "Why" before "How".
- Communication is a core part of the job.
- DevOps is not about being a hero.

**What makes a great DevOps engineer:**
- Tools change. Fundamentals stay.
- Be curious and keep learning.
- Think in systems, not just tasks.
- Automate boring and repeatable work.
- Use AI to leverage skills, not to turn off your brain.

#### Speaker 2: Đạt Phạm & Cường Nguyễn – "From Intern to MNC: Real Stories from Data Analytics & Corporate Culture"

**What a Data Analytics Engineer intern does:**
- Build daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly reports to track operational performance.
- Design dashboards to manage data trends, detect anomalies, and support business decisions.
- Analyze business/operational KPIs, find root causes, and propose improvements.
- Collaborate cross-functionally to solve real business problems.

**Case studies at real companies:**
- **Kamereo:** Build reports to track company GMV trends, identify weak points and propose improvements.
- **Colgate-Palmolive:** Analyze machine, operation, and IoT device data in the factory; support smart factory digital transformation initiatives.

**Key skills for a Data Analytics Engineer:**
- **Critical Thinking:** Objective information analysis to draw sharp insights.
- **Communication Skills:** Clearly presenting analytical results to all stakeholders.
- **Storytelling with Data:** Transforming numbers into meaningful narratives that drive action.
- **Problem Solving:** Identifying challenges and finding data-driven optimal solutions.

**Career development model (5 stages):**
1. **Follower:** Executes tasks step-by-step as instructed by seniors. Focuses on building foundational skills.
2. **Learner:** Starts understanding solutions but still needs mentor guidance. Asks deep questions to accumulate hands-on experience.
3. **Problem Solver:** Proactively analyzes business problems, proposes optimal solutions, and commits to quality output.
4. **System Thinker:** Views problems from a wide, holistic perspective. Anticipates operational risks and optimizes the system long-term.
5. **Super Star:** Builds vision, defines comprehensive data strategy, and develops the next generation of System Thinkers.

**Corporate culture at MNCs:**
- **Recruitment process at MNCs:** ATS scan → English phone screen → aptitude test (logic/situation) → technical interview (STAR model) → cultural fit interview.
- **No-Blame Post-Mortem culture (MNC Tech):** Engineers focus on finding root causes in the system rather than blaming individuals when a serious incident occurs.
- **Caring & Inclusive culture (MNC FMCG):** Puts people at the center of development; builds an environment that respects diversity and continuous improvement.
- **Global lessons from Japan & Korea:** Japan's Wakon Yosai (Japanese spirit + Western knowledge) and Korea's Chaebol model that strictly adheres to international standards.

### Key Takeaways

#### Career Mindset
- **Start with fundamentals:** Tools change, but foundational knowledge (Linux, networking, programming) remains essential. Mastering fundamentals beats chasing tools.
- **Career growth is staged:** The Follower → Super Star model applies to both DevOps and Data roles — focus on growing your competency at each stage, not just chasing titles.
- **Community matters:** The FCAJ community is a real launching pad — from student programs to AWS Community Builder to professional AWS Partners.

#### Technical Knowledge
- **DevOps is about systems thinking**, not just tools. Understanding *why* before *how* is the key differentiator.
- **Data engineers must tell stories**, not just build dashboards. The ability to translate data into decisions is the most valued skill.

#### Professional Development
- **Communication and cross-team collaboration** are as important as technical skills in MNC environments.
- **Build real projects early:** Deploy apps, automate something, monitor it, break it — this is how real learning happens.
- **Use AI as a force multiplier,** not a replacement for deep understanding.

---

### Applying to Work

- **Build a small DevOps project:** Set up a simple CI/CD pipeline (GitHub Actions), containerize an application, and add basic monitoring.
- **Practice storytelling with data:** In my next reporting task, go beyond presenting numbers — identify root causes and propose a clear recommendation.
- **Get involved in the FCAJ community:** Engage more actively in community events, consider applying for the AWS Student Builder Group Program.
- **Apply the career development model:** Honestly assess which stage I am at (Follower/Learner/Problem Solver) and set clear goals for reaching the next stage.
